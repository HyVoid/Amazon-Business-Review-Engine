# Quick Start Workflow

The workbook is designed around a simple operational principle:

**Configure once. Import new reports. Refresh periodically.**

No formulas need to be copied.
No reports need to be rebuilt.
No dashboards need to be recreated.

### 1. Configure Reporting Parameters

Open the **Config_Param** worksheet and define the reporting configuration.

Typical parameters include:

- Analysis period (Weekly or Monthly)
- Current reporting period
- Comparison period
- SKU-to-Parent ASIN mappings
- Optional reference tables such as exchange rates or product groups

These settings normally remain unchanged after the initial setup.

---

### 2. Import Existing Reports

Export reports from existing operational systems such as:

- Amazon Seller Central
- Amazon Search Query Performance (SQP)
- Sellerboard

Paste each CSV directly into its corresponding raw data worksheet.

No manual column rearranging.

No reformatting.

No preprocessing.

The workbook automatically standardizes incoming data into a consistent analytical structure.

---

### 3. Review Results Instantly

Select **Refresh All** in Excel (or refresh the Browser version).

The analytical engine automatically updates:

- Executive KPI dashboard
- SKU performance analysis
- Parent ASIN summaries
- Keyword opportunity analysis
- Weekly / Monthly / Year-over-Year comparisons
- Executive trend charts
- Top performance gains and losses

No manual calculations are required.

---

### 4. Refresh on a Regular Schedule

Each reporting cycle follows exactly the same workflow:

Export reports.

Replace the raw data.

Refresh.

Review the updated business insights.

There is no need to rebuild formulas, recreate Pivot Tables, redesign charts, or configure dashboards again.

---

# Why I Built This

Every Amazon seller exports reports.

Very few have a reporting process.

In most businesses, the weekly review begins with downloading CSV files from multiple systems, adjusting column formats, deleting unnecessary fields, rebuilding formulas, recreating Pivot Tables, and copying charts into PowerPoint. By the time the data is finally ready, the meeting is already focused on explaining numbers instead of understanding the business behind them.

The real bottleneck isn't data availability—it's the lack of a repeatable analytical framework.

I built this project after seeing the same pattern repeated across different Amazon businesses. Every week, analysts spent hours preparing reports that were nearly identical to the previous week's. Every month, comparison periods had to be rebuilt manually. Every quarter, historical trends became increasingly difficult to reconcile because each spreadsheet evolved independently.

The problem wasn't Excel.

The problem was that Excel was being used as a document instead of an analytical engine.

This project productizes the reasoning process behind an Amazon Business Review. Instead of treating every reporting cycle as a new spreadsheet, it standardizes data preparation, comparison logic, KPI calculation, and performance diagnosis into a reusable framework.

### Before

Sales dropped by 8%.

The team manually compared multiple reports trying to determine whether the decline came from advertising efficiency, shrinking search demand, inventory issues, or underperforming products. Hours were spent collecting evidence before meaningful discussion could begin.

### After

The dashboard immediately identifies:

- Three SKUs contributing over 70% of the revenue decline.
- Two high-volume keywords with significant Purchase Share losses.
- Stable advertising efficiency despite reduced traffic.
- Declining market search volume as the primary driver of lower sales.

Instead of asking *"What happened?"*, the discussion starts with *"How should we respond?"*

That is the purpose of this project—not to automate spreadsheets, but to standardize business reasoning into a repeatable decision-support system.

# Three Traps That Catch Even Experienced Amazon Analysts

---

## Trap 1 — Sales Declined, Therefore Advertising Failed

### Decision

Advertising budget is increased because weekly sales declined.

### Hidden Problem

Revenue alone does not explain why sales changed.

Traffic, conversion rate, inventory, pricing, and market demand may all produce similar revenue changes.

### Incorrect Reasoning

| Observable Metric | Conclusion |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Sales -8% | Increase PPC budget |

The recommendation assumes advertising caused the decline.

### Correct Analysis

The engine decomposes revenue movement into:

- Search demand
- Click-through performance
- Conversion performance
- Purchase share
- SKU contribution

Example:

| Metric | Result |
|---------|--------|
| Search Volume | -11% |
| Brand CTR | Stable |
| Brand CVR | Stable |
| Purchase Share | Stable |

Recommendation:

> Demand shrank.
>
> Increasing advertising budget will not recover market volume.

Correct decision:

Maintain advertising efficiency while protecting profitability.

<details>
<summary>Formula Logic</summary>

```excel
Revenue Change
=Cur_Revenue-Pri_Revenue

Contribution
=SUMIFS(...)

Search Volume Comparison
=SUMIFS(...)

Purchase Share Comparison
=SUMIFS(...)
```

</details>

---

## Trap 2 — The Lowest Profit SKU Must Be Removed

### Decision

A low-profit SKU is discontinued.

### Hidden Problem

Absolute profit is different from profit contribution.

Removing one SKU may reduce traffic that supports other products.

### Incorrect Analysis

| SKU | Profit |
|------|---------|
| A | $320 |
| B | $40 |

Conclusion:

Remove SKU B.

### Correct Analysis

The workbook ranks SKUs by **profit change**, not current profit.

Example:

| SKU | Profit Change |
|------|---------------|
| A | -$12,500 |
| B | +$200 |

Recommendation:

SKU A deserves investigation.

SKU B is improving.

Correct decision:

Prioritize investigation using contribution ranking instead of absolute values.

<details>
<summary>Formula Logic</summary>

```excel
Profit Change

=Cur_Profit-Pri_Profit

Ranking

=RANK(
ProfitChange,
NegativeChanges,
1
)
```

</details>

---

## Trap 3 — High Search Volume Means High Opportunity

### Decision

Advertising budget is increased on the highest-volume keyword.

### Hidden Problem

Search volume measures market demand.

It does not indicate whether the listing is competitive.

### Incorrect Analysis

| Keyword | Search Volume |
|----------|---------------|
| coffee grinder | 480,000 |

Conclusion:

Increase bids.

### Correct Analysis

The workbook compares:

- Brand CTR
- Market CTR
- Brand CVR
- Market CVR

Possible diagnosis:

| CTR | CVR | Recommendation |
|------|------|----------------|
| Low | High | Improve main image or pricing |
| High | Low | Improve listing content |
| Low | Low | Reduce bidding until listing improves |

This prevents spending advertising budget on structural listing problems.

<details>
<summary>Formula Logic</summary>

```excel
CTR Gap

=BrandCTR-MarketCTR

Opportunity

=IF(
BrandCTR<MarketCTR,
Improve Image,
Improve Listing
)
```

</details>

# Example Scenario

A brand selling kitchen accessories exports weekly reports from Amazon Seller Central, Sellerboard, and Search Query Performance.

The reporting period is **2026-W27**.

After replacing the raw CSV files and selecting **Refresh All**, the workbook automatically rebuilds the standardized fact tables.

Executive dashboard summary:

| KPI | W26 | W27 | Change |
|-----|------|------|--------|
| Revenue | $105,200 | $120,450 | +14.5% |
| Net Profit | $24,000 | $31,500 | +31.2% |
| PPC Spend | $26,000 | $25,100 | -3.5% |
| ACoS | 24.71% | 20.84% | Improved |

SKU diagnostics identify:

- One SKU generated 46% of total profit growth.
- Two Parent ASINs account for nearly all incremental revenue.
- Three underperforming SKUs explain most remaining losses.

Keyword diagnostics identify:

- Two high-volume keywords lost Purchase Share.
- CTR remains above market average.
- Conversion rate declined because competitor listings improved.

Business recommendation:

Instead of increasing advertising spend across all campaigns, prioritize listing optimization on the two declining keywords while maintaining existing PPC efficiency.

The reporting process takes only a few minutes and produces a repeatable executive-ready business review without rebuilding formulas or Pivot Tables.

# Validation Rules

| Field | Rule | Error Behavior |
|--------|------|----------------|
| Analysis_Type | Weekly or Monthly only | Validation message |
| Current_Period | Valid reporting period | Dashboard not refreshed |
| SKU | Cannot be blank | Excluded from Fact table |
| Parent ASIN | Must exist in mapping table | Displays "Unmapped" |
| Date | Valid Excel date | Row excluded |
| Sales Revenue | Numeric | Returns zero |
| Net Profit | Numeric | Returns zero |
| Search Volume | Integer ≥ 0 | Validation warning |
| Purchase Share | Between 0% and 100% | Validation warning |
| CTR / CVR | Percentage | Validation warning |
| Duplicate Date + SKU | Automatically aggregated | No manual action required |
| Blank Raw Rows | Ignored automatically | No error |

</details>
